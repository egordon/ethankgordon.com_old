Research / Curricular

Photonic Neural Network Design, Control, and Simulation

This independent project investigates the design and control of photonic neural networks. I focused on the thermal control and calibration of network components, as well as demonstrating that the network dynamics were sufficient to solve standard non-linear classification problems.

Additionally, as a component of this thesis, I created LightLab, a general laboratory simulation environment. All calibration and training procedures in this thesis could be tested on simulated laboratory instruments. This allowed for good agreement between simulated and empirical results.

  • Poster
  • Paper

LightLab is currently closed-source by Princeton.

Ball Catcher

An quasi-independent project (due to some unforeseen circumstances) for Car Lab (ELE 302), Ball Catcher was designed to track and catch a thrown projectile. I had to design and build the chassis and holonomic drivetrain to maximize the catch radius, design and wire four H-Bridges for responsive current deliver, and implement a controller to predict the landing position of the projectile and guide the robot in that direction.

  • Source/CAD
  • Paper

Implementation and Design is Open-Source. Presented at Princeton.

Integrated Power-Efficient Quantum Randomness

In this partner project, we designed an integrated system to utilize quantum randomness in linear optics. Compared to classical pseudo-random schemes, we determined that this system (along with other similar systems) is not power efficient for a practical number of bits with current single-photon avalanche diode technology.

  • Slides
  • Paper

Final investigation was not published due to negative results.

CoinJoin

A partner project for the Cryptocurrencies Graduate Seminar (COS 597E), this is an implementation of the CoinJoin algorithm. It takes advantage of the multi-output property of bitcoin transactions to grant true anonymity instead of just pseudonymity.

  • Source

P-Auth: Hands-Free Authentication

P-Auth allows users to authenticate based on eye movements using the face recognition API of Project Oxford. Users first register by training a face with a single picture. They can then login by sending two pictures of their face that differ only through pupil movement in a random direction as dictated by the server.

  • Source

Open-Source. Recognized at HackPrinceton.

BTC Oracle

BTC Oracle provides a means to create a cryptographically enforced contract based on arbitrary conditions written in python. We also developed a local application that runs alongside your existing bitcoin wallet (bitcoind) and provides an interface to the oracle itself. More information about oracles can be found on the Bitcoin Wiki.

  • Source

Open-Source. Presented at HackPrinceton.
